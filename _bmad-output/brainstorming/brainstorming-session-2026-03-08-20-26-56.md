## Idea Organization and Prioritization

**Thematic Organization:**

### Identity and Trust Surface

- **Trust Surface:** The website's primary job is to increase respect and confidence in Chris across professional and personal audiences without turning into a self-promotional billboard.
- **Person-First, Builder-Second:** The site should communicate the person first and let craftsmanship and technical execution reinforce that impression.
- **Multi-Audience Legibility:** Hiring managers, clients, coworkers, curious people, recruiters, and investors should all find a credible path through the site.

### Information Architecture and Content Model

- **Three-Principle Architecture:** The site's long-term spine is `personal identity`, `things I make`, and `signals of life`.
- **Narrative Homepage Flow:** The preferred homepage arc is `who I am -> what I make -> what's happening now -> where to go next`.
- **Depth by Invitation:** The homepage should remain curated and uncluttered, with deeper pages carrying detail.

### Visual Direction and Editorial Feel

- **Countercultural Editorial:** Borrow underground magazine energy without clutter or uninformed hot takes.
- **Notebook Authenticity:** Borrow intimacy and truth-seeking presence without illegibility.
- **Elegance Without Elitism:** Borrow gallery spaciousness and cleanliness without coldness or stuffiness.

### Freshness and Publishing Strategy

- **Freshness Concentrator:** The homepage should make it obvious what is new without requiring exploration of every corner.
- **Unified Update Surface:** Short posts and media may share a lightweight publishing model.
- **Aggregation as Support Layer:** External activity should enrich the site, but fail gracefully and not become a brittle runtime dependency.

### Launch Scope and Product Roadmap

- **Launch Now:** Homepage, About, Projects, Resume/Experience, Contact, `Uses This Site`, external project links, and social/profile links.
- **Launch Later:** `/now`, writing index, photos index, project subpages, timeline, search, and newsletter.
- **Hybrid Launch Strategy:** Ship a complete trust-first core, then add richer liveness and editorial systems.

### Technical Architecture and Operations

- **Operations-First Stack:** Prioritize operational simplicity, cost safety, uptime, security, and calm ownership.
- **Static Core, Dynamic Edge:** Use a mostly stable core with additive dynamic features like aggregation and code-free editing.
- **Code-Free Vital Edits:** Resume, homepage featured items, projects, short posts, and media entries should be editable without changing application code.

- **Why This Matters:** Success is defined by visitors coming away with a higher opinion of Chris on both professional and personal levels.
- **Immediate Next Steps:**
1. Define the trust signals the first 30 seconds must communicate: human clarity, technical competence, professionalism, taste, and substance.
2. Draft a short homepage identity statement and a supporting paragraph for About/hero use.
3. Select the trust-critical homepage modules: introduction, selected work, current activity, external ecosystem links, and clear next steps/contact.
- **Resources Needed:** Resume, accomplishment notes, current project list, external site links, profile photo
- **Timeline:** 2-4 days
- **Success Indicators:** The site feels credible, personal, and non-salesy; a visitor quickly understands who Chris is and why he is worth paying attention to.

### Priority 2: Three-Principle Architecture

- **Why This Matters:** This keeps the site coherent over time and prevents page sprawl or homepage clutter.
- **Immediate Next Steps:**
1. Map all launch pages and homepage modules to `personal identity`, `things I make`, or `signals of life`.
2. Write the initial sitemap and define what each page contributes.
3. Maintain a disciplined `not now` list for deferred pages and features.
- **Resources Needed:** Brainstorming output, desired page list, launch/later scope split
- **Timeline:** 1-2 days
- **Success Indicators:** Every page has a clear reason to exist; homepage stays curated; future growth remains additive.

### Priority 3: Distinct Visual Direction

- **Why This Matters:** Distinctive design is central to making the site memorable, personal, and impressive without feeling gimmicky.
- **Immediate Next Steps:**
1. Create a reference board around underground editorial, gonzo notebook, and accessible gallery influences.
2. Translate those references into practical design rules for typography, spacing, imagery, texture, motion, and writing voice.
3. Create a short `borrow vs reject` design brief to preserve discipline during implementation.
- **Resources Needed:** 8-15 reference examples, example copy, profile imagery, a basic visual-language document
- **Timeline:** 2-5 days
- **Success Indicators:** The visual language feels recognizably Chris, timeless rather than trendy, and supportive of trust and clarity.
