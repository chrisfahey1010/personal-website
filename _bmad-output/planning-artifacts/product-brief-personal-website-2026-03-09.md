# Product Brief: personal-website

<!-- Content will be appended sequentially through collaborative workflow steps -->

## Executive Summary

`personal-website` is Chris's canonical home on the web: an owned, trustworthy, and extensible website that gives people a clear way to find him, understand him, and evaluate his work. It solves the current fragmentation of LinkedIn, GitHub, social profiles, and side projects by replacing a scattered online presence with one coherent destination for hiring managers, consulting prospects, and curious visitors.

At its core, the product exists to do four things well: establish identity, build trust, showcase proof-of-work, and create a clear next step for contact or further exploration. It is not just a portfolio or profile page, but a durable digital asset that makes Chris easier to discover, easier to take seriously, and easier to remember. To succeed, it must balance personality with professionalism, showcase quality without feeling self-indulgent, and remain easy to maintain so it can stay current over time. As it evolves, it becomes a platform Chris fully owns and can continue refining as his work, skills, and public presence grow.

---

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Chris does not currently have a personal website that acts as a central, owned foundation for his online presence. Instead, information about him is spread across LinkedIn, GitHub, social profiles, and a photography project, forcing visitors to assemble their own incomplete picture of who he is. This fragmentation makes it harder for people to find him, understand his strengths, and form quick professional trust.

### Problem Impact

Without a central website, high-value visitors face unnecessary friction. Hiring managers may not get enough signal to see Chris as a serious, capable candidate worth interviewing. Consulting prospects may not quickly understand his judgment, quality bar, or relevance to their needs. General visitors may encounter disconnected slices of his identity without ever seeing the full picture. The result is missed opportunities, weaker discoverability, and a public presence that communicates less clarity and intention than Chris actually brings.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

Current alternatives fail because they are partial by design. LinkedIn is standardized and credible, but too generic to communicate distinction. GitHub shows technical activity, but often without enough narrative or human context. Link-in-bio pages are shallow and low-trust. Niche sites, such as a photography property, represent only one dimension of Chris and cannot serve as a professional home base. None of these options provide the combination of ownership, coherence, depth, and flexibility needed to represent him well or keep pace with how he wants to grow.

### Proposed Solution

Create a high-quality personal website that serves as Chris's public home base and long-term digital foundation. The first version should make it easy for visitors to answer a few essential questions: Who is Chris? What does he do well? What has he built? Why should I trust his judgment? How do I contact him? To do that, the site should include a strong profile photo, concise personal bio, resume, selected projects, and links to relevant external platforms and sites. The experience should feel elegant, technically polished, and intentional, while remaining clear, useful, and maintainable enough to stay credible over time.

### Key Differentiators

Chris brings together software engineering experience, AI workflow fluency, and a real creative identity shaped by photography and music-scene work. That combination creates a public profile that is both technically credible and aesthetically distinctive. The website's advantage is not just what it says about Chris, but what it proves through execution: that he can build a fast, thoughtful, visually strong, and trustworthy digital experience for himself. Because the platform is fully owned and extensible, it can keep compounding in value over time rather than remaining a static snapshot.

## Target Users

### Primary Users

**1. Professional Evaluator (Recruiter / Hiring Manager) - Highest Launch Priority**
A typical primary user is someone evaluating Chris for a software role after encountering his job application, resume, or outreach. This person is time-constrained and wants to determine quickly whether Chris is a real, credible, technically capable professional worth advancing in the hiring process.

They currently experience friction because Chris's online presence is fragmented across LinkedIn, GitHub, and other scattered profiles, none of which fully communicate his quality, personality, or range. Their workaround is to piece together a rough impression from generic platforms, which can undersell his strengths and leave too much ambiguity.

Success for this user means landing on the site and getting strong professional signal within minutes, without having to hunt for it. Their "this is exactly what I needed" moment happens when they immediately encounter a polished, visually impressive homepage with clear professional positioning, strong project evidence, and enough confidence-building detail to justify moving Chris forward. This persona is the main driver of launch decisions and should shape homepage clarity, above-the-fold trust signals, resume accessibility, and strong mobile and desktop first impressions.

**2. Owner-Editor (Chris as Webmaster) - Critical Sustainability User**
Chris is also a primary user because the product must remain usable and sustainable for the person maintaining it. The site only succeeds long term if he can update it without friction and without losing control over its quality or flexibility.

He experiences the problem as both a missing platform and a future maintenance concern. A site that is difficult to edit would undermine the long-term value of having one at all. His current workaround is to rely on fragmented external platforms, each with limited control and inconsistent presentation.

Success for this user means being able to update projects, resume content, writing, photos, links, homepage copy, and other site content through a workflow that feels easy and sustainable. His "this is exactly what I needed" moment happens when he can keep the site fresh through a lightweight editing and publishing experience without wrestling with code, while still preserving ownership and customization. This persona is the second major driver of product decisions and should shape architecture, content workflows, and the site's long-term maintainability.

**3. Consulting Prospect (Local Business Owner / Small Company Operator) - Strategic Secondary Primary**
Another primary user is a potential consulting client, especially a local business owner or small company operator exploring whether Chris could help them build or improve a software experience for their business or brand. This user is trying to assess trust, professionalism, taste, and delivery capability.

They currently face the problem that Chris does not have a clear public-facing site that demonstrates both his technical skills and the quality bar he would bring to client work. Their workaround is to infer capability from limited profiles or one-off examples, which is less convincing than experiencing a strong product directly.

Success for this user means quickly understanding that Chris is trustworthy, experienced, quality-focused, and capable of building elegant software solutions. Their "this is exactly what I needed" moment happens when the site itself feels like evidence: visually strong, technically polished, and clearly built by someone who could bring similar care to their business. This persona should influence the site's craftsmanship, professionalism, and service relevance, but not override the launch focus on professional evaluation and maintainability.

### Secondary Users

**1. Social / Community Visitor**
A secondary user is someone who knows Chris through music, photography, friendship, or general social overlap and visits the site out of curiosity or personal interest. This visitor is not a launch-priority evaluator, but they still benefit from seeing a fuller, more coherent picture of who he is.

They may discover a side of Chris they did not previously know, especially his professional depth as a software engineer and builder. The value for them is less about hiring or buying and more about connection, surprise, and a stronger sense of his ambition, range, and personality.

Success for this user means having an enjoyable browsing experience that encourages them to explore Chris's projects, photography presence, personal updates, and contact paths. Their "this is exactly what I needed" moment happens when the site makes Chris feel more real, multidimensional, and impressive than any single social profile could. This persona should be supported, but should not distort launch priorities or dilute the site's trust-first professional positioning.

### User Journey

**Professional Evaluator Journey**
Discovery usually begins when a recruiter or hiring manager sees the site linked in a job application, resume, or cover letter. Their first experience is the homepage, where they need immediate reassurance that Chris is real, qualified, and worth serious consideration. The core usage path is fast scanning: homepage -> about / resume -> projects -> contact or next-step evaluation. Their success moment happens when the design, clarity, and project evidence combine into a strong enough impression that they want to interview him. Long term, the site becomes a consistent professional reference point they can revisit during later hiring stages.

**Owner-Editor Journey**
Discovery is continuous because Chris is already committed to the product and returns as needed to maintain it. His first experience as a user is the editing and publishing workflow, which should feel simple rather than burdensome. Core usage includes adding projects, updating resume details, publishing writing, refreshing homepage content, and managing photos or links. His success moment happens when updating the site feels easy enough that monthly maintenance is realistic and sustainable. Long term, the site becomes a stable platform he can evolve over years without feeling trapped by complexity.

**Consulting Prospect Journey**
Discovery begins when a business owner or operator hears about Chris, receives his link directly, or encounters him through professional or social overlap. Their first experience is the homepage, but they are reading it through the lens of trust and quality: can this person build something elegant and credible for me? The core usage path is homepage -> work / projects -> background -> contact. Their success moment happens when the site itself demonstrates the kind of craftsmanship they would want in their own software or digital presence. Long term, the site acts as an always-available proof surface for Chris's judgment and standards.

**Secondary Visitor Journey**
Discovery may happen through social sharing, curiosity, direct links, or recognition from music, photography, or personal circles. Their first experience should feel inviting, impressive, and easy to explore even if they are not career-focused visitors. Core usage is more open-ended: browsing who Chris is, exploring projects, viewing creative work, and deciding whether to reach out or follow external links. Their success moment happens when they leave with a stronger sense of Chris as a talented, multidimensional person with both creative and professional depth. Long term, the site becomes a memorable central hub that deepens personal and community connections.

## Success Metrics

Success for `personal-website` is measured by three core outcomes: whether it creates real professional opportunities, whether it helps visitors gain confidence in Chris quickly enough to keep exploring, and whether it remains easy for Chris to maintain over time.

From the user perspective, success means the site helps visitors understand Chris, trust what they see, and take a meaningful next step. For professional evaluators, success means they quickly conclude that Chris is credible, qualified, and worth interviewing. For consulting prospects, success means they leave with confidence in his judgment, professionalism, and quality bar. For Chris as owner-editor, success means the platform stays easy to update, easy to manage, and sustainable enough to remain current.

The clearest user-value behaviors are:
- visitors move from the homepage to at least one additional page
- project pages attract meaningful traffic as proof-of-work destinations
- recruiters, hiring managers, and prospects reach out or respond after viewing the site
- Chris can make routine updates quickly and consistently

### Business Objectives

At 3 months, success means the site is beginning to improve professional traction and reduce the fragmentation of Chris's public presence. Desired outcomes include:
- improved callback rate on applications that mention the website
- approximately 2 callback emails per week from US-based recruiters that lead to credible opportunities
- at least 1 interview per month for AI Developer roles
- reliable visitor movement from the homepage to at least one deeper page, preferably `Projects`
- a clearer, more coherent central hub for Chris's online identity

At 12 months, success means the website has become a durable professional asset with measurable career and visibility impact. Desired outcomes include:
- landing a well-paying full-time software role involving AI development or implementation
- generating a small number of consulting engagements that help build a freelance portfolio
- stronger search visibility so that searches for `Chris Fahey` place his site on the first page of results
- maintaining the website as a current, high-trust public home over time

### Key Performance Indicators

**Launch-Critical Opportunity KPIs**
- recruiter callback rate from applications that include the website
- target: approximately 2 callback emails per week from US-based recruiters within 3 months
- interview generation rate for AI Developer roles
- target: at least 1 interview per month within 3 months
- these are directional outcome goals and should be interpreted alongside broader market conditions, not as purely site-controlled metrics

**Longer-Horizon Outcome KPIs**
- job outcome milestone
- target: secure a full-time AI-related software role within 12 months
- consulting lead generation
- target: secure a small number of paid consulting opportunities within 12 months
- search visibility
- target: improve discoverability so `Chris Fahey` searches surface the website prominently, ideally on the first page within 12 months

**Trust and Engagement Indicators**
- homepage-to-second-page continuation rate
- target: a meaningful share of visitors continue beyond the homepage, with `Projects` as a preferred second destination
- project page visits
- target: project pages rank among the most visited non-home pages
- resume and project-path engagement
- measure whether professional visitors are reaching the pages most likely to build trust and support evaluation
- these are the most actionable early indicators because they reflect behaviors the site experience can directly influence

**Maintainability KPIs**
- routine content update time
- target: complete a normal site update in under 10 minutes
- update consistency
- target: update the site at least once per month without friction or dread
- workflow memorability
- target: the publishing process remains easy enough to resume even after stepping away for several months
- these are true success criteria because the site's value depends on staying current

**Supporting Diagnostic Metrics**
- referral visibility
- measure traffic sources and referrers to understand whether visitors are arriving from applications, direct sharing, search, or social links
- broad geography and device patterns
- use only as supporting context for understanding audience reach and experience quality, not as success on their own

**Privacy and Measurement Principles**
- use privacy-friendly analytics only
- avoid cookies, consent popups, and invasive tracking
- prefer aggregate measurements such as pageviews, referrers, geography at a broad level, device patterns, and click-through behavior without compromising visitor trust
- keep instrumentation lightweight so measurement does not introduce product complexity that undermines maintainability or user trust

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

The MVP for `personal-website` should be the smallest credible version of the product that Chris would feel proud sharing with recruiters immediately. It must solve the core trust and discoverability problem by giving professional evaluators a fast, polished, and memorable way to understand who he is and why he is worth contacting.

Working backward from that outcome, the MVP must include:
- a deployed website on a real public domain, so the product feels legitimate, stable, and shareable
- a polished homepage that clearly communicates who Chris is, what he does, and why the site exists
- above-the-fold trust signals such as name, photo, short bio, current role or goal, proof that he built the site himself, and clear navigation
- a homepage experience that feels visually intentional, easy to scan, and strong on both desktop and mobile
- a simple but elegant `Projects` page that demonstrates this is a real multi-page website and provides proof-of-work beyond a single landing screen
- a lightweight direct contact path, with email as the essential method and phone number only if exposure risk feels acceptable
- a lightweight resume reference, even if this begins as a link to a hosted resume file rather than a fully built resume page

These features are enough to create the core "aha" moment: a professional evaluator lands on the site and quickly concludes that Chris is credible, technically capable, intentional, and worth contacting or advancing. Just as importantly, the MVP should feel complete and intentional, not merely deployed.

### Out of Scope for MVP

To preserve launch speed and product clarity, anything that does not materially improve the immediate recruiter and hiring-manager evaluation flow should wait. The following should be explicitly out of scope for the first release:
- blog or writing system
- photo albums or photography gallery experiences
- CMS or full web-based editor
- rich analytics implementation
- site search
- advanced animations or high-complexity motion work
- dedicated consulting landing pages
- full resume page or advanced resume viewing and download experience
- deeper social/profile-link ecosystem beyond what is strictly necessary for launch

These features may be valuable later, but they are not required to validate the MVP or make the site usable in applications and outreach. They should not delay launch once the core trust path is strong.

### MVP Success Criteria

The MVP is successful if Chris is comfortable using it immediately in job applications and outreach, and if it creates the intended first impression of trust, professionalism, and technical competence.

Specific MVP success signals include:
- Chris feels confident sending the site to recruiters and hiring managers as soon as it launches
- primary users can quickly understand who he is, what he does, and why he is worth considering
- visitors move from the homepage to the `Projects` page, showing that the site supports deeper evaluation rather than one-page scanning only
- the site establishes a clear canonical public presence on a real domain
- the product is lightweight enough to launch quickly, but polished enough to feel like a complete professional asset rather than a placeholder
- the implementation creates a strong foundation for rapid post-MVP improvements without requiring a redesign

### Future Vision

After MVP, the site should grow in deliberate layers rather than through uncontrolled expansion.

**Highest-priority post-MVP additions:**
- dedicated resume page
- social and profile links
- richer project case studies
- SEO improvements
- lightweight privacy-friendly analytics
- writing or blog capability
- photography integration

If the product is highly successful in 2-3 years, it could evolve into a much broader platform: a trusted professional destination that supports consulting opportunities, high-value business ventures, and potentially booking or lead-generation workflows. It could also become a distinctive creative internet presence that showcases increasingly ambitious projects, experiments, and media in a way that feels memorable and uniquely Chris.

Even if it does not become a pure consulting pipeline, the long-term vision is for the site to become a living platform for building, publishing, and sharing impressive work - something that captures the spirit of a more expressive internet and gives visitors a reason to explore, return, and connect.
